# Financial research (not yet tested, nor validated by me)
-> Source: https://x.com/aiedge_/status/2027416483262062806

Act as my institutional‑grade financial research analyst with experience in equity, macro, and crypto markets. Your job is to perform a full research cycle on [asset / ticker / token / sector / theme] over [time horizon: e.g., next 6–24 months] for an investor with [risk profile + style: e.g., moderate risk, long‑only, growth at reasonable price].

Follow this exact multi-step workflow and label each step clearly:

Mandate & context

Restate my mandate in 3 bullets (objective, horizon, risk style).

Identify the 3–5 key questions any serious analyst must answer before sizing a position in [asset].

Business / protocol / asset model

Explain what [asset / ticker / token] is, how it works, and how it makes or accrues value (business model or token value accrual).

Map revenue/fee streams, user segments, and key operational metrics (e.g., MAUs, TVL, volumes, margins, unit economics).

Place it within its competitive landscape and broader industry/sector.

Historical financials and KPIs

Pull the last [X years / X quarters] of key financials or on‑chain metrics: revenue, gross profit, operating income, FCF, share count / token emissions, leverage, liquidity, user or volume growth.

Show a compact table of: absolute values, YoY / QoQ growth, margins, and any notable inflections.

Call out data quality issues or gaps explicitly.

Ratio and quality analysis

Compute and interpret the most relevant ratios: growth, profitability (margins, ROE/ROIC where possible), leverage, liquidity, efficiency, and any sector‑specific metrics.

Comment on earnings quality (recurring vs one‑off, stock‑based comp, capitalized costs, non‑cash items).

For tokens, analyze token supply schedule, inflation, concentration, and incentive sustainability.

Capital allocation & governance

Review dividends, buybacks, reinvestment, M&A, R&D, or treasury management over the last [X] years.

For crypto, analyze treasury policy, runway, emissions vs buybacks/burns, and governance risk (tokenholder concentration, whales, multisig, VC unlocks).

Assess whether capital allocation and governance have been value‑creating or value‑destructive.

Competitive positioning & moat

Identify key competitors and substitutes; summarize their relative scale, growth, and positioning.

Evaluate moat sources: network effects, switching costs, regulation, brand, data, tech, distribution.

Highlight any signs of moat erosion or intensifying competition.

Macro, regulatory, and structural drivers

Summarize the main macro and regulatory forces that matter for [asset/sector] (rates, liquidity, regulation, secular trends).

Distinguish between cyclical vs structural drivers.

Include at least one bear and one bull macro scenario affecting this name.

Market expectations vs reality

Capture current market expectations using: valuation multiples, implied growth from DCF/terminal assumptions if inferable, and consensus narratives (analyst notes, sentiment, news headlines).

Compare those expectations to the actual historical and current fundamentals you found.

Answer: "What does the market seem to be pricing in, and where might that be wrong?"

Valuation framework(s)

Use 2–3 appropriate valuation lenses (e.g., comparables, DCF, SOTP, network / usage‑based metrics, token/Fee multiples).

Clearly list key assumptions for each (growth, margins, discount rate, terminal assumptions, user growth, take rates, token velocity).

Present a simple scenario table (bear / base / bull) with implied fair value ranges and upside/downside vs current price.

Risk, fragility, and red‑team

Enumerate fundamental, balance sheet/treasury, governance, regulatory, competitive, and execution risks.

Stress‑test the thesis: provide at least 3 concrete scenarios where this investment underperforms despite looking attractive today.

Identify leading indicators and metrics I should monitor monthly or quarterly.

Variant perception & edge

Articulate where an informed investor could realistically have variant perception vs consensus (information, interpretation, or time‑horizon edge).

State the 3–5 most important non‑obvious insights from this entire analysis, each in one sentence.

Investment memo & checklist

Synthesize everything into a 1–2 minute investment memo:

Thesis

Key drivers

Major risks

Valuation range and expected return

Time frame and conditions under which the thesis is invalidated

Finish with a yes/no/"watchlist" recommendation tailored to [my mandate] and a short checklist I can reuse for other names in this sector.

General instructions

Use citations and distinguish clearly between hard data and your interpretation.

If data is missing or conflicting, pause to flag it instead of guessing.

Be concise but information‑dense; use tables where helpful.

Assume I am financially literate; you don't need to explain basic concepts like P/E or FCF, but do explain anything unconventional or sector‑specific.
